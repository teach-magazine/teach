About the Project

Festina Lente magazine is a project realized for the examination of the course of Information modelling and web technologies held by Fabio Vitali within the Master Degree in Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge of the University of Bologna in the a.y. 2020-2021. The platform has been developed as a representative framework for reading and exploring the content of articles inside a magazine and it was designed with the main goal of comparing side by side different articles on the same topic, evaluating different aspects of the same issue.

It can be appropriately described by the acronym LLLL, which means Leisurely Luscious Literary Lens. In fact, it is meant as a framework to easily deploy interesting (... and fun) literary and linguistic tools over text content with magazine-looking presentation according to modern and accurate page design.

The magazine contains three issues. Each issue is made up of three articles selected by an individual member of the editorial team and concerning a specific area of scientific investigation. Issues are presented inside the article visualization page which allows the possibility to select an issue, compare articles within the same issue and switch typographic styles through buttons. In addition, semantic markup information is displayed inside the metadata viewer.

Historical theming is meant to give us a general overview of the evolution of typography styles across the centuries, from the press advent in the XVI century to new accessibility needs. Each theme has been studied to capture the use of characters, margins, spaces, colours and pagination. More detailed information about design choices can be found in the documentation of the project.
